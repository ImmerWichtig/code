Mahatma Gandhi

The founder of Satyagraha doctrine, father of the Bharat nation.

“Be the change that you wish to see in the world.” - Mahatma Gandhi

The Life of Mahatma Gandhi

  • 1869: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born into a Gujarati Hindu Modh Bania family. His father was a chief minister of a princely state of British Raj, Porbandar.
  • 1878: Enrolled school in Rajkot. 2 years later enrolled Alfred High School.
  • 1883: Married Kasturba Gandhi in an arranged marriage.
  • 1888: Graduated from high school, and started studying law and jurisprudence in Inner Temple, London
  • 1891: Called to the bar, left London for India.
  • 1893: Set sail for South Africa to become a lawyer. Spent 21 years there.
  • 1906: Adopted Satyagraha, which means 'devotion of the truth'.
  • 1915: Returned to India with the international reputation as a leading Indian nationalist, theorist and community organiser. Also joined Indian National Congress, a political party in India.
  • 1920: Took leadership of the Congress, made demands to the Brits for the independence of India.
  • 1930: Marched 388 kilometres from Ahmedabad to Dandi as a protest against the British salt tax.
  • 1942: Started Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India.
  • 1947: Achieved independence of India, however dismayed by the religious violence due to the partition of British India.
  • 1948: End of life of a hero, assasinated by a Hindu nationalist.
